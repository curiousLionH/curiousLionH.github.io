## Bio

I am a Research Engineer at Hyundai Motor Company's Autonomous Driving Division, where I develop perception systems for autonomous vehicles — including multi-object tracking (MOT), sensor calibration, and LiDAR-based perception pipelines. I am concurrently pursuing an **Online M.S. in Computer Science at Georgia Institute of Technology** (GPA: 4.00/4.00), with a master's project on 3D point cloud registration using diffusion models (targeting NeurIPS 2026).

My work sits at the intersection of **3D perception**, **multi-sensor fusion**, and **deep learning for autonomous driving**. I am broadly interested in robust and generalizable methods for understanding the 3D world from sensor data.

---

## Research Interests

- **3D Perception & Point Cloud Processing**: registration, segmentation, object detection
- **Multi-Object Tracking**: end-to-end MOT, evaluation metrics, data association
- **Multi-Sensor Fusion**: LiDAR, camera, RADAR, IMU calibration and integration
- **Generative Models for 3D**: diffusion-based approaches for geometric tasks

## Research

**Master's Project: 3D Point Cloud Registration** · Aug 2025 – May 2026  
*Georgia Institute of Technology*  
Contributed to a coarse-to-fine framework for rigid and non-rigid point cloud registration, designed for robustness under noise, partial overlap, and deformation. Involved in architecture design, baseline evaluation pipeline development, and inference optimization. Working toward a submission to **NeurIPS 2026**.

**AMLAB — 3D LiDAR-Based MOT for Autonomous Vehicle Perception** · Dec 2021 – Feb 2022  
*Automotive Mechatronics Lab, Sungkyunkwan University*  
Designed and implemented a full LiDAR perception pipeline (RANSAC ground removal, voxel downsampling, DBSCAN clustering, 3D bounding box extraction) and a Kalman-filter-based tracker with Hungarian assignment. Integrated multi-sensor data (LiDAR, RADAR, camera, IMU, GPS) in ROS 2.
